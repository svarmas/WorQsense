    Legally compliant but procedurally deficient is still a liability

    Most IC members know the PoSH Act. Few know how to conduct a defensible inquiry. The gap is where organisations are exposed, in court, appellate review, and board scrutiny.

The inflection point

Why this matters now.

PoSH obligations maturing

After a decade, regulators and courts are examining process quality, not just compliance. Weak investigations are now a documented liability.

GRC mandate under the new labour codes

The Industrial Relations Code extends structured grievance handling to a far broader class of employers. The obligation is coming before most organisations are ready.

Scrutiny rising

Tribunals now examine how an investigation was conducted, not just what it concluded.

Improvisation no longer defensible

Ad-hoc responses create legal, ethical, and reputational exposure. Good intentions are not a substitute for process.

When to engage

Is this your situation?

WORQsense handles PoSH Act 2013, GRC under IR Code 2020, misconduct, conduct, and employment matters, and Section 10 conciliation when the complainant requests it.

A PoSH complaint has been received

The 90-day deadline is running. The IC is untested. Or the complaint involves a senior leader. Each of these changes what the inquiry needs to be.

A GRC matter requires investigation

Grievance Redressal Committee matters under the IR Code 2020 require independent investigative capability, separate from PoSH, and equally demanding of process rigour.

Internal capacity is compromised

The internal team knows the parties. Or leadership is implicated. Or the IC has never run an inquiry before. Independence is not optional when proximity is a fact.

A prior investigation is being challenged

A previous inquiry is subject to court challenge, appellate review, or board questioning. An independent assessment of process and findings is required.

Conciliation has been requested

Under Section 10 of the PoSH Act, the complainant may request conciliation before an inquiry. This requires a credentialled process. Sreevidya Varma is an AMINZ-credentialled mediator.

ERA exposure (New Zealand)

A personal grievance, dismissal, or disciplinary matter requires a defensible process under the Employment Relations Act 2000.
